Privacy

Last updated 1 May 2026.

Train is a one-person workout tracker. The shorter this page is, the more accurate it is — here's the whole picture.

What we collect

  • Your Google account email and name, via Sign in with Google. We use the email to identify your account; the name is shown on the settings page.
  • The workouts you log — sets, reps, weights, exercise preferences, timestamps, and the dark/light theme you've chosen.
  • If you use the contact form: whatever message you type, the optional reply email you provide, and your IP address (used for rate-limiting submissions). The message is delivered to me by email; nothing about contact submissions is stored in the app database.

Where it lives

All of it sits in a SQLite database on a single small server in Australia. There's no cloud data warehouse, no third-party analytics, no ad network, no tracking pixels. Backups are taken on the same server.

What we don't do

  • No analytics or tracking of any kind.
  • No advertising.
  • No selling, sharing, or transferring your data to anyone else.
  • No reading your data ourselves except to fix bugs you've reported.

Cookies

Two cookies, both first-party: a session cookie so you stay signed in, and a theme cookie remembering whether you prefer dark or light mode. That's the lot.

Deleting your data

Email james67@gmail.com asking for deletion and your account and every workout under it will be removed, usually within a day or two.

Changes

Any changes to this policy will be posted on this page with the "last updated" date above bumped accordingly.
